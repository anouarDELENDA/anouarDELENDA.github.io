In materials engineering, defects such as dislocations (e.g. screws and edges) are of
fundamental importance. The control of these defects has the potential to drive significant advancements in the field of materials science. However, these defects are not yet fully understood, especially in the case of anisotropic
materials. My research focuses
on understanding the behaviour of these defects and their impact on layered materials such as graphite. To this end:

- I am focusing on dislocations defects (initially screws) and their impact on layered
    materials (primarily graphite).
- I use both Gaussian ([AIMPRO](http://aimpro.ncl.ac.uk)) and Plan-wave ([VASP](https://www.vasp.at)) DFT-based code
- I perform large scale molecular dynamics simulations ([LAMMPS](https://www.lammps.org/#gsc.tab=0)) using machine learning interatomic
    potential.
